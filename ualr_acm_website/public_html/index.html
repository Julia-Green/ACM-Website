"Advancing Computing as a Science & Profession"

Founded in 1947, the Association for Computing Machinery is the world's largest computing Society. As the UA Little Rock student chapter of the ACM, our mission is to be a resource for the academic and professional development of our members and to advance the interests of the field of computing within our community.

Need a computer?

The UA Little Rock ACM Student Chapter has identified some of the difficulties being faced by Students across the nation. Currently, students are having to adapt to a new, online learning environment due to the COVID-19 virus. We see that there is a need for access to computing devices for students taking classes online and feel that this is an issue that we are well equipped to help our local community overcome. In September 2020, we ran a tech donation drive and had over 100 computers donated to us. We have refurbished as many of these computers as possible and have them available for students and families that are in need. If you are in need of a computer, please feel free to complete our computer request form!

This membership option provides all of the aforementioned benefits with the added bonus of full access to the ACM Digital Library. The ACM Digital Library provides access to all ACM publications, text publications from certain publishers, and the ACM Guide to Computing Literature database.
