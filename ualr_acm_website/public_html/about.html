About The ACM

Founded in 1947, the Association for Computing Machinery is the world's largest computing Society. As the UA Little Rock student chapter of the ACM, our mission is to be a resource for the academic and professional development of our members and to advance the interests of the field of computing within our community.

Our Mission

We strive to equip students in their pursuance of computing education in such a way they are excited to learn.

Events

We host frequent events, whether it be guest speakers from industry, educational activities, or just to work on projects and hangout.

Involved

We are involved in our communities. Whether it be other ACM chapters, the home University, or generically the community of learning. We have a presence so we can help make a change when it is needed most!

Come check out our clubroom!

Our clubroom, located in EIT 565, is open to all chapter members. The space houses multiple tables and workstations for members to eat, hang out, work, or study. We store our technology here, which is available for all members to use and experiment with. Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, we have had to limit in-person access to our clubroom for the time being. One of our members has created a 360 virtual tour of our clubroom for people who haven't been able to visit it in person. You can check it out here!
